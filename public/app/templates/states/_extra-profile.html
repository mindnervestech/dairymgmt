You may also add credit to your years of specific experience per the following guidelines:
  • Technical Certification (industry recognized) = +3 months experience
  • Associate degree in CS, IS, Engineering, Business or related technical discipline = +18 months experience
  • Bachelor’s degree in CS, IS, Engineering, Business or related technical discipline = +3 years experience
  • Master’s degree in CS, IS, Engineering, Business or related technical discipline = +2 years experience plus credit for BS
  • Doctorate in CS, IS, Engineering, Business or related technical discipline = +2 years experience plus credit for BS and MS
  • General years of experience may be added to specific years of experience at a ratio of 3 years general to 1 year specific
  • Specific experience is work experience related to the position
  • Internships do not count towards specific or general work experience

Instructions

In this section, list detailed descriptions for each individual project role you have held. When completing this section, it is essential to keep the following points in mind:
  • Follow the format exactly as shown.
  • Any lapses in employment of more than 1 month should be addresses (e.g., "Student," "Unemployed," "Leave of Absence," etc.)
  • Use the most recent job title for each company.
  • Use separate bullet points for each assignment or project. Include: date range, project role or job title, and descriptions of responsibilities that are thorough, yet concise.
  • Ensure no gaps exist between project roles. Address gaps between projects, such as awaiting clearance, proposal support, etc.
  • Ensure patterns of short­term employment are addressed (i.e., held job for fewer than 18 months or held 3 or more jobs in a five year period). This only applies to positions held within the past 15 years.
  • Written consistently in the first or third person.
  • Appropriate verb tense is used­ present tense for you current assignment, past tense for previous assignments.
  • Spell out the first instance of all abbreviations.
